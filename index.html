Mission

Crypto for Nature

The Problem of unrecycled Plastic Bottles causes a very severe issue for the Planet as most of the plastic bottles available for retail purchase are made from PET or polyethylene terephthalate. This is an ideal material for making single-use water bottles as it is strong, lightweight, and a type-1 plastic, which means it can be easily recycled. The recycling process has several stages; collecting bottles from a central location, then shredding them with other PET products, melting the shreds down, and reforming them into pellets. These pellets then act as the raw material for the production of new plastic goods, including new water bottles! There are many benefits to recycling plastic bottles and, across the globe, governments are taking steps to encourage both businesses and consumers to do this. For example, there are recycling programs running in several US states, (such as California, Massachusetts, and Wisconsin) which either encourages or makes mandatory, the commercial recycling of plastic bottles and other waste products. to spend funds on the Recycling of waste plastic bottles that are single used and thrown which might harm the environment and nature.
